As a Developer:

  • Come to our monthly meetups,
  • Volunteer to speak at our monthly meetups by contacting the committee,
  • Write an article for the Jozi-JUG online community,

For recruiters:

Please, this is for those who program Java, NOT for recruiters, NOR staffers, NOR head hunters, career creators etc. While we do love what you do, this place is a sanctuary for us Java Developers.

As a Sponsor:

We are always looking for new sponsors and venues. Please feel free to contact the committee.

The Jozi-JUG is a not for profit initiative run by volunteers and attended by software professionals that give up their private time to better their skills. The Jozi-JUG recognizes that Java developers are scarce, but we ask that it's members not be contacted for the purpose of employment without explicit consent from the individual developer. The Jozi-JUG believes that by being a sponsor promotes the sponsors brand among the attending Jozi-JUG developers.

What will a sponsor get

  • The sponsor will be displayed on http://meetup.com/Jozi-JUG/ with a email sent to all it's members.
  • The sponsor can put up as much branding as they choose.
  • The sponsor can have a talking slot for up to five minutes to welcome the people attending.
  • The sponsor my very briefly indicate that they are looking for developers.
  • Brand visibility and exposures to the sponsors premises.

What to expect

  • The Jozi-JUG generally meets on the last Monday of each month, except for December which may be earlier in the month. When there is a public holiday on the last Monday, the next suitable Monday is selected.
  • The Jozi-JUG has two sessions at it's monthly meetups that target different skill levels. Two separate rooms are required with projectors and seating.
  • The Jozi-JUG usually announces it's next meetup just after it's previous meetup. This is just slightly shorter than one month before the event.
  • We are unable to guess the number of attendees on announcement, but our average turnout is about 50 in total.
  • As the time grows closer to the event , we can better guesstimate the numbers. The sponsor may ask us at any time for a guesstimate.
  • One week before the time we will send a formal guesstimate.
  • Typically a RSVP does not equal a person attending, so don't be alarmed if the guesstimate is lower than the final RSVP count.
  • We typically expect food at our venues provided by our sponsors. If this is not possible , please make arrangements with the organizers before the meetup is announced.
